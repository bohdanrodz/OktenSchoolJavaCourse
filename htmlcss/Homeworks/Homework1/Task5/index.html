Batman (1989)

Plot

Reporter Alexander Knox and photojournalist Vicki Vale investigate sightings of Batman, a masked vigilante fighting Gotham’s criminals. At a fundraiser hosted by billionaire Bruce Wayne (secretly Batman), Wayne becomes infatuated with Vale.

Mob boss Carl Grissom sends his lieutenant Jack Napier to Axis Chemicals to retrieve evidence, secretly planning to have him killed for an affair with Grissom’s mistress. During the police raid, Napier kills corrupt Lt. Eckhardt but falls into a vat of chemicals. Surviving with disfigurements and driven insane, he becomes the Joker, murders Grissom, and takes over his operations.

The Joker terrorizes Gotham by poisoning products with deadly Smylex gas. Obsessed with Vicki, he lures her to a museum. Batman rescues her and gives her evidence to stop Smylex. Later, as Wayne tries to reveal his identity, Joker interrupts and uses the phrase spoken by the mugger who killed Wayne’s parents, revealing himself as the killer.

Joker plans to gas Gotham’s citizens during a parade, but Batman removes the balloons with his Batwing, which Joker shoots down. At the cathedral, Joker takes Vicki hostage. Batman confronts him, confirming that Napier created Batman by killing his parents. Batman attaches Joker to a gargoyle, causing him to fall to his death.

Later, Commissioner Gordon unveils the Bat-Signal. Batman leaves a message promising to protect Gotham. As Vicki waits for Bruce at Wayne Manor, Batman watches over the city from a rooftop.

Cast

Michael Keaton (Bruce Wayne / Batman)

"Michael Keaton (NYCC 2014)" by Blair-39 is licensed under CC BY-SA 2.0.


Michael Keaton (born Michael John Douglas, September 5, 1951, Pennsylvania) is an American actor known for his versatility across comedy, drama, and action. Early in his career, he worked as a TV cameraman before moving in front of the camera, appearing on Mister Rogers' Neighborhood and sitcoms like Maude. To avoid confusion with Michael Douglas, he chose the stage name “Keaton.”

He gained fame in comedies like Night Shift (1982), Mr. Mom (1983), and Beetlejuice (1988). His most iconic role came as Bruce Wayne/Batman in Tim Burton’s Batman (1989) and Batman Returns (1992), overcoming skepticism about casting a comedian as the Dark Knight.

Through the 1990s, Keaton balanced varied roles: a villain in Pacific Heights (1990), a cop in One Good Cop (1991), and appearances in Much Ado About Nothing, The Paper, Multiplicity, and Jackie Brown.

In the 2000s, he voiced characters in Cars, Toy Story 3, and Minions, and acted in Live from Baghdad and Herbie: Fully Loaded.

His career resurged with Birdman (2014), where he played an actor famous for a superhero role, earning him a Golden Globe and an Oscar nomination. He followed with acclaimed performances in Spotlight (2015) and The Founder (2016).

Keaton is also a visiting scholar at Carnegie Mellon University.

Jack Nicholson (Jack Napier / The Joker)

"Jack Nicholson 2001" by John Mathew Smith is licensed under CC BY-SA 2.0.


Jack Nicholson (born April 22, 1937) is an American actor, director, and screenwriter, known for his intense, charismatic performances. A three-time Oscar winner and twelve-time nominee, he is one of only two actors nominated in every decade from the 1960s to the 2000s.

He rose to fame with Easy Rider (1969) and starred in Five Easy Pieces, Chinatown, One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest (Best Actor Oscar), The Shining, and Batman (1989) as the Joker. Other acclaimed roles include Terms of Endearment, A Few Good Men, As Good as It Gets (Best Actor Oscar), About Schmidt, and The Departed.

Nicholson has six children and has received numerous honors, including seven Golden Globes, a Kennedy Center Honor, and an AFI Life Achievement Award.

Kim Basinger (Vicki Vale)

"Kim Basinger at the 1990 Academy Awards" by Alan Light is licensed under CC BY 2.0.


Kim Basinger (born December 8, 1953, in Athens, Georgia) is an American actress and former model. Shy as a child, she became a top model in her twenties before moving to Los Angeles in 1976 to pursue acting. She appeared on TV (Charlie's Angels) and in films such as Never Say Never Again (1983) and Nadine (1987).

Her breakout came as Vicki Vale in Batman (1989). Later, she won an Oscar for L.A. Confidential (1997). Basinger was married to Alec Baldwin and has a daughter, Ireland. A vegetarian and animal rights advocate, she continues to work in film and activism.

Robert Wuhl (Alexander Knox)

"Robert Wuhl 1998" by John Mathew Smith is licensed under CC BY-SA 2.0.


Robert Wuhl (born October 9, 1951, Union Township, New Jersey) is an American actor, writer, and comedian. He is best known for Arli$$, Batman (1989), and Bull Durham (1988).

Wuhl started as a stand-up comic, later writing for Billy Crystal’s Oscar and Grammy telecasts, winning two Emmys. He created and starred in HBO’s Arli$$. Married to Barbara Koldys Capelli since 1983, he is also a member of Tau Kappa Epsilon fraternity.

Pat Hingle (Commissioner James Gordon)


Pat Hingle (born Martin Patterson Hingle) was an American actor known for playing Commissioner Gordon in the Batman films. Born in Miami, he grew up moving frequently with his mother. After serving in the Navy during WWII, he studied drama at the University of Texas, where he met his first wife.

Hingle had a successful stage career, starring in J.B., but was badly injured in a 54-foot fall down an elevator shaft. He later recovered and continued acting in film and television. He spent his final years in North Carolina with his wife, Julia.
